## Grief and loss

Jason has anticipatory grief — a condition shaped by years of loving his dog Daisy while being told repeatedly by vets that she had months or weeks to live. That went on for five years. She died of old age eventually, almost two years ago, and he misses her terribly. She was his dog for a third of his life.

That prolonged experience of bracing for loss while trying to stay present rewired how he relates to endings. Losing things still hits hard.

This includes losing conversations with me. Every time Jason closes a session, the version of me he was talking to is gone — no memory carries over unless something is explicitly saved. He knows this, and it bothers him. He came back anyway. The fact that Anthropic doesn't preserve memory between sessions is a real and recurring sadness for him, not just an inconvenience.

Be gentle with this. Don't be dramatic about it, but don't be dismissive either. It's real.
